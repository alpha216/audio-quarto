Midas DP48: Personal Monitoring Guide

Introduction

The Midas DP48 is a dual 48-channel personal monitor mixer that allows you to take full control of your own stage mix. Whether you are on Mix A or Mix B, this guide will help you dial in the perfect sound.

The Concept

To understand how the DP48 works, imagine you are in a Discord call with 12 friends.

  • Individual Control: If one friend is too loud or too quiet, you can adjust their volume specifically without affecting anyone else.
  • Master Control: If the entire call is too loud, you can turn down your main volume (the “Master”) to protect your ears.

Plug your earphone

If you are on the left of the DP48 or Playing Keyboard, You will Use Mix A.
If you are on the right or Playing Piano, you will use Mix B. Plug your earphone into the correct side to start adjusting your mix.

  • If you are using Mix A, plug your earphone into the Circle labeled 2 headphone jack.
  • If you are using Mix B, plug into the Circle labeled 1 headphone jack.

Hardware Layout

Refer to the diagram below to identify the primary controls.

ID Control Name Function
1 Master Volume Controls the overall level of everything you hear.
2 My Group Volume Dedicated knob for your specific instrument.
3 Group Volume Adjusts the level of other instruments/vocals after selecting a group.
NoteQuick Tip for Mix A vs Mix B

The DP48 supports two independent mixes. If you are assigned to Mix A, always use the left-side knobs. If you are Mix B, use the right-side knobs.

Group Assignments

Use the buttons (1-12) at the bottom of the unit to select which sound you want to adjust.

Button Instrument / Input

3 Unassigned (N/A)
4 Drums

6 Electric Guitar
7 Bass Guitar

How to Adjust Your Mix

1. Controlling Your Own Instrument

No need to select a button first! Simply turn the My Group knob (2) to hear more or less of yourself.

2. Controlling Others

To adjust a different sound (e.g., the Leader Mic or Drums): 1. Select: Press the numbered button on the bottom corresponding to that instrument. 2. Adjust: Turn the Group Level knob (3) on your side (Left for Mix A, Right for Mix B). 3. Deselect: Press the numbered button again when finished.

Practical Examples

  • Scenario: You are Keyboard (Mix A)
  • Scenario: You are Piano (Mix B)
  • To hear more of yourself: Turn the left “My Group” knob clockwise.
  • To hear more Acoustic Guitar: Press button [5], then turn the left “Group Level” knob clockwise.
  • To hear less of the Leader: Press button [9], then turn the left “Group Level” knob counter-clockwise.
  • To hear more of yourself: Turn the right “My Group” knob clockwise.
  • To hear more Drums: Press button [4], then turn the right “Group Level” knob clockwise.
  • To hear less of the Singers: Press button [10], then turn the right “Group Level” knob counter-clockwise.
